The PC devolopment

First Computer

The first computer that resembled the modern machines we see today was invented by Charles Babbage between 1833 and 1871. He worked on it for nearly 40 years. used in public uses.

First gen Computer

The German engineer Konrad Zuse enabled the creation of the first binary programmable computer in history in 1938, which he named Z1. A year later, in 1939, the American physicist John Atanasoff and engineer Clifford Berry built an analog computer known as the Atanasoff Berry Computer (ABC).

Second Gen Computer

The Second Generation of computers relied on transistors as their primary component. The Transac S-2000, manufactured in 1958 by Philco Corporation, is considered one of the first computers to utilize transistors."

Third Gen Computer

The emergence of third-generation computers can be attributed to the development of the integrated circuit (IC), invented by Robert Noyce and Jack Kilby between 1958 and 1959. This marked the beginning of modern computing. The IBM-360 is considered one of the most significant third-generation computers, with IBM investing nearly $5 billion in its production.

Fourth Gen Computer

The invention of the microprocessor led to the 4th generation of computers. The Intel 8008, introduced in 1972, was the first microprocessor. Microprocessors significantly reduced the cost of producing computers, leading to the emergence of personal computers (PCs), laptops, and mobile phones. The Altair 8800, IBM 5100, and Micral are examples of early fourth-generation computers.

Fifth Gen Computer

Fifth-generation computers emerged in 2010, and they rely heavily on artificial intelligence(AI). These computers can interact with natural language inputs, learn, and self-organize, exhibiting a level of intelligence that resembles human intelligence. IBM's Watson computer is one of the most famous examples of fifth-generation computers.
